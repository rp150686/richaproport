The AI Lens

Where AI is actually changing the compliance floor

Richa's view, formed from years inside the alert queue rather than a vendor deck: AI doesn't replace the KYC analyst's judgment — it changes what the analyst spends that judgment on. The gains show up in the noise, not the decision.

01 / TRIAGE

Machine learning thins the queue before a human touches it

Anomaly-scoring models on transaction monitoring can rank alerts by genuine risk instead of raw rule-hits, so investigators spend time on the matches likely to be true — which is exactly the mechanism behind the false-positive gains she's driven in past roles.

02 / SCREENING

Fuzzy-matching & NLP cut through name-screening noise

Entity resolution and natural-language processing over adverse media and sanctions lists catch transliteration and alias variants that rigid rule-based screening misses — while still routing every genuine hit to a human for disposition.

03 / GOVERNANCE

Explainability is the price of admission, not a nice-to-have

A model a regulator can't interrogate is a model compliance can't defend. Any AI in the sanctions or SAR workflow needs an audit trail as rigorous as the one built for human decisions — FATF, FCA and RBI expectations don't relax for automation.

04 / THE ANALYST

The job shifts from searching to judging

As AI absorbs the repetitive matching work, the analyst's value moves toward escalation calls, narrative-writing on SARs, and reading intent behind ownership structures — the parts of the job that still resist automation.
